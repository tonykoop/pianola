# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A pianola-style player-piano mechanism shown as a finished review prototype and real physical object: an upright wooden piano case with its front panel removed to reveal the player action, a cream perforated paper music roll mounted on a wooden take-up spool and feeding down across a slim metal tracker bar, the paper visibly tracking over the bar's row of small sensing ports. Behind the tracker bar sits a serviceable valve chest of small leather-and-cloth pneumatic pouches and pilot valves, connected by neat rows of soft rubber and lead tubing that fan upward to pneumatic actuators coupling to the back of the conventional key and hammer action. Materials read as warm aged piano-cabinet wood with a satin lacquer finish, brass and nickel fittings, black felt and leather pneumatic parts, and matte tubing; realistic scale cues come from the full-height upright case and the roughly hand-width paper roll. Clean neutral studio background, soft directional studio light, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

The same player-piano mechanism staged in a quiet instrument-maker's workshop, front panel off and a short perforated test strip threaded across a single tracker-bar port mid-motion, a removable pouch-and-pilot-valve module sitting open on the bench for inspection beside coils of leak-check tubing, a piano action test rail, and hand tools. Warm natural daylight rakes across the wood grain and the row of tracker-bar ports, shallow depth of field holding the tracker bar and pneumatic stack in focus while the workshop blurs softly behind. Careful prototype atmosphere with serviceable access on display; concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of the signature mechanism: the cream perforated paper roll crossing a smooth metal tracker-bar port, the row of small open sensing ports visible through and beside the holes in the paper, with tiny tubes leading to a supple leather pouch and valve seat and a pneumatic actuator linkage just behind. Emphasize textures — fibrous paper edge, machined metal port row, soft leather and felt pouch, matte tubing, visible service screws and clean sealing surfaces — under raking light that picks out each port and the subtle curl of the paper. Shallow depth of field, high detail, photorealistic, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, fake maker marks, or fake tune names; no dimension callouts or measured hole spacing; no impossible paper paths or pneumatics connected to nothing, no piano keys moving without action linkage, no steampunk clutter; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
