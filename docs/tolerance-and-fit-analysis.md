# Tolerance And Fit Analysis

Status: open-question analysis. No numeric clearances are released.

## Roll To Tracker Bar

Open questions:

- How much paper wandering can occur before a hole opens the wrong tracker port?
- What contact condition seals the paper without dragging or tearing it?
- How should edge guidance avoid creating wear or skew?
- What inspection method detects paper debris at ports before false triggering?

## Tracker Port To Tubing

Open questions:

- How isolated must adjacent ports be before cross-talk is inaudible or
  mechanically irrelevant?
- What tube or channel routing keeps signal delay consistent enough for later
  timing tests?
- What connection method allows cleaning and replacement without changing port
  authority?
- What leak test distinguishes tracker leakage from downstream valve leakage?

## Pouch And Valve Fit

Open questions:

- What pouch material and mounting method prevents sticking after rest?
- What valve seat condition seals reliably while still venting quickly?
- How much valve travel is needed before response is repeatable?
- What return behavior confirms the valve is ready for another roll event?

## Actuator To Piano Action

Open questions:

- What handoff point in the piano action should the pneumatic actuator move?
- How much lost motion can exist before timing or tone becomes unreliable?
- What stop or decoupler prevents damage if a valve stays open?
- How should the actuator be removed without disturbing piano regulation?

## Service Fits

Open questions:

- Which covers must be removable before tracker cleaning is possible?
- Which pneumatic components must be replaceable without unthreading the whole
  system?
- How should test ports be placed so leak isolation is practical?
- What labels or witness marks are needed without implying final geometry?
